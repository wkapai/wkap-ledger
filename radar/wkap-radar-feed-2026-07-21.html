THESIS_SUMMARY

VICR enters earnings with a market expectation that has already changed
materially after the company raised its Q2 revenue outlook.

The opportunity is not simply an earnings beat. The more important question
is whether management can show that Vertical Power Delivery, royalty
income, licensing, Gen2 customer ramps, and new manufacturing structures
are creating a more scalable and higher-margin business.

This is both a fundamental thesis and a short-duration earnings trade. The
near-term price response will likely depend more on forward evidence than
on the reported quarter alone.
------------------------------
WKAP_ANGLE

The surface-level frame:

“VICR is a volatile power-component company entering a high-expectation
earnings report.”

The alternative frame:

“VICR may be transitioning into an AI power-delivery toll collector with a
growing royalty and licensing component.”

The key research question:

“Will this earnings call provide enough customer, backlog, royalty, and
capacity evidence to support a business-model rerating?”

------------------------------
CORE_THESIS

AI accelerators are moving toward current requirements above 1,000 amps,
making conventional lateral power delivery increasingly inefficient and
thermally constrained.

Vicor’s Vertical Power Delivery architecture places power modules directly
beneath the processor, potentially reducing power-delivery-network
resistance and improving current density. The strategic thesis is that
power delivery becomes a critical performance constraint as accelerators
scale.

The larger potential rerating comes from the business model. Management has
discussed a combination of internal manufacturing, Fab2, jointly owned
facilities, licensing, royalties, and patent enforcement.

If licensing and royalty revenue scale faster than manufacturing
requirements, VICR could become less capital-constrained and structurally
more profitable than a conventional component supplier.

However, much of the multi-billion-dollar revenue and 70% gross-margin
scenario remains aspirational and requires significant verification.

design-win stickiness.

production, and the amount of silicon per vehicle can rise as ADAS, cabin
monitoring, radar, vision, connectivity, and electrification increase.

relevant beyond vehicles.

relationships are real and economically meaningful, the market may begin
treating INDI as a Physical AI perception platform rather than only an
automotive chip supplier.

The problem is timing. The company’s reported revenue growth remains
modest, GAAP losses continue, and the balance sheet carries meaningful debt
and dilution risk.

CORE_THESIS

Thunder is designed as a Group 5 autonomous aircraft rather than a small
tactical drone.

The platform combines series hybrid-electric propulsion with dual
tiltrotors, allowing vertical takeoff, wing-borne cruise, longer endurance,
and potentially lower acoustic visibility.

The strategic logic is that Archer’s commercial eVTOL work has already
forced it to develop electric propulsion, flight controls, aircraft
integration, testing infrastructure, and manufacturing processes.

Anduril contributes autonomy, defense systems, mission architecture, and
access to military procurement.

If the platform can reuse Archer’s existing technology base while receiving
defense-funded development support, the company may gain a second path to
monetization before commercial air-taxi adoption reaches scale.

The economic evidence remains incomplete. Thunder’s own first flight is
planned for 2027, and customer announcements have not yet established
funded production demand.
