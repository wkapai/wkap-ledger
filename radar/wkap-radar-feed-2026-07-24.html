that part of the retail base continued averaging down.

Goldman Sachs Prime data show global semiconductor net exposure rising
from *10% to 24%*, then declining to *19%*. U.S. exposure moved from *7%
to 14%*, then back to *11%*. Positions remain around the *97th and 96th
five-year percentiles*, respectively.

*ATTENTION_ENVIRONMENT*

Attention is shifting from sector beta toward second-order bottleneck
plays.
-

*WKAP_VIEW*

The Korean leveraged-ETF number is best interpreted as a reduction in the
mechanical selling base—not proof that investors have surrendered their
positions.

Similarly, hedge-fund semiconductor exposure has declined from the June
peak, but remains historically crowded. Lower selling intensity can support
a rebound in memory, equipment and packaging, yet it does not automatically
create inexpensive entry conditions.

This market is more likely to reward company-specific evidence than broad
semiconductor exposure. Near-term priority should remain on earnings,
customer commitments and margin conversion rather than assuming that the
entire sector has entered a new up-cycle.

long-term quality than headline results imply.

*Themes/tickers:* GOOGL, Search advertising, AI CapEx, free cash flow, LLM
cannibalization

advertiser ROI or higher monetization per increasingly lower-quality click?”
