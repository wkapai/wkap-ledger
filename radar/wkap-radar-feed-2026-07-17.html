WKAP_ANGLE

The surface-level frame:

AI-related product momentum.”

“MXL may be developing into a broader AI connectivity platform, with
optical DSP, TIA, SerDes, and high-speed interconnect products changing
both its growth profile and its comparable-company set.”

The key research question:

“Does the July 23 report show that AI optical growth is broadening across

CORE_THESIS

MaxLinear supplies connectivity and mixed-signal silicon across broadband,
infrastructure, industrial, and data-center markets. The current rerating
thesis is not simply that AI creates more semiconductor demand. It is that
MaxLinear’s revenue mix may be shifting toward products that sit directly
inside high-speed data-center optical connectivity.

The first-quarter evidence is meaningful:

-

Revenue reached $137.2 million, up 43% year over year.
-

Infrastructure revenue increased 136% year over year.
-

Infrastructure became the company’s largest end market.
-

Management said optical products were ramping at multiple hyperscale
customers.
-

Second-quarter revenue was guided to $160 million–$170 million.

The company has also positioned products such as its Rushmore 1.6T DSP for
both scale-up and scale-out AI connectivity. This supports the possibility
that MXL participates in more than one layer of the data-center
interconnect architecture.

If the thesis works, the market may stop valuing MXL primarily as a
cyclical communications supplier and begin using a higher-growth AI
infrastructure framework. The rerating would require not only revenue
growth, but also evidence that optical products are diversified across
customers, sustain attractive margins, and remain relevant beyond the
current product cycle.

THESIS_SUMMARY

XFAB is entering Radar because the company’s rapidly growing wide-bandgap
and industrial businesses may be obscured by continued automotive inventory
digestion.

First-quarter industrial revenue increased 32% year over year, wide-bandgap
revenue increased 152%, and SiC wafer shipments increased 195%. Management
explicitly cited growing data-center power-management activity, but
automotive revenue still fell 10%, and wide-bandgap revenue remains small
relative to the company’s total business.

The July 30 report is the immediate event. This is an event-driven rerating
candidate with a real fundamental foundation, but the AI narrative is not
yet large enough to stand independently from the automotive cycle.
------------------------------
WKAP_ANGLE

The surface-level frame:

“XFAB is a European automotive and industrial specialty foundry waiting for
the auto semiconductor cycle to recover.”

The alternative frame:

“XFAB may be an underfollowed physical-infrastructure supplier to AI data
centers through SiC, GaN development, power management, mixed-signal
technologies, and other specialty processes.”

The key research question:

“Can data-center power and wide-bandgap growth become financially material
before automotive weakness and underutilization consume the operating
leverage?”

------------------------------
CORE_THESIS

AI data centers create power-conversion and thermal-management challenges
that cannot be solved solely through faster GPUs or optical links. Higher
rack power, higher-voltage distribution, and repeated voltage conversion
increase the value of efficient power semiconductors and specialized analog
processes.

XFAB operates specialty foundries serving automotive, industrial, medical,
and communications applications. Its relevance to AI infrastructure is
indirect but economically plausible: it manufactures power-management and
wide-bandgap components rather than designing headline AI processors.

The first-quarter evidence supports the beginning of this mapping:

-

Industrial revenue reached $52.0 million, up 32% year over year.
-

Wide-bandgap revenue reached $15.1 million, up 152%.
-

SiC wafer shipments increased 195% year over year and 28% sequentially.
-

Management cited strong SiC demand and increasing data-center
power-management activity.
-

Automotive revenue remained much larger at $121.6 million but declined
10%.
-

Backlog was $308.4 million, down 3% sequentially.

The potential mispricing is therefore not that the market has entirely
missed XFAB’s AI exposure. It is that the market may be assigning almost no
strategic value to a business that could compound quickly from a small base
while automotive comparisons stabilize.

If the thesis works, three things should occur together:

1.

Wide-bandgap and industrial revenue remain strong.
2.

Automotive declines moderate.
3.

Higher utilization creates visible margin improvement.

Without all three, the AI power narrative may remain too small to change
the company-level earnings framework.
